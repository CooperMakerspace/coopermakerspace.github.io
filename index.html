The Cooper Union Makerspace:
A learning lab & idea hub!

The Makerspace is a learning lab that serves Cooper's Engineering student body & the STEM Outreach programs. The lab is an interdisciplinary space that houses 3D printers, a lasercutter, and a myriad of other tools to support your project-making. Come by during open hours to utilize the workspace for rapid prototyping and assembly, have a chat with the operators or coordinator and find the learning resources to achieve your project's goals! Please see below for print and cut requests, as well as workspace norms.

- Norms, Tips & Resources!-

When you visit we invite you to respect the following code of conduct:

  • There are many exciting tools in here that require PPE:
    o When soldering, the smoke extractor must be used.
    o When drilling/hand-sawing/scoring/snapping acrylic or wood, goggles must be worn.
    o When using an adhesive other than PVA and hot glue, wear gloves for mixing/application AND use a snorkel above your work area.
  • Practice being explicit:
    o If you are not ABSOLUTELY sure how to use a tool, ASK.
    o If you are injured, or see someone else getting injured, SAY IT OUT LOUD.
  • Materials are here for your use but let us know!
    o If you would like to use a consumable item or component that will not be returned, you must fill out the Makerspace Item Sign-out sheet.
    o If you would like to borrow a Makerspace item for use outside of the Makerspace, you must fill out the Makerspace Item Sign-out sheet.
  • When you leave your work area and the Makerspace:
    o If you are using/used a multimeter, power supply, oscilloscope, glue gun or soldering iron do not forget to TURN OFF & UNPLUG.
    o Return tools to their designated storage area.
    o Clean up after yourself & carry out your belongings, including your project.

Some tips for 3D printing:

  • Very tall objects will take more time and have a greater chance of failing.
  • Very thin parts are unable to print.
  • The format of the file sent should be STL saved in mm.
  • Anything that has an angle 45 degrees or larger, in reference to the x-axis, will not need support when being printed.
  • When creating holes, the tolerance should be 0.8 mm greater for the inner diameter.
