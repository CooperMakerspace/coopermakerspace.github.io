- Our STEM Programs -

Summer Stem

Summer Stem

Summer STEM offers six week programs for high school sophomores and juniors and three week programs for high school freshman in design, engineering and hands-on problem-solving. Class projects cover topics from current problems in civil, chemical, electrical, and mechanical engineering. Students gain knowledge in an engineering field and guidance to address a real-world problem by designing and engineering a team project.

Saturday Stem

Saturday Stem

Taught by Cooper Union undergraduates and graduate students,students learn engineering design and rapid prototyping skills, including 3D drawing and printing, laser cutting, fabrication with hand tools, and programming microcontrollers. Students work in teams design, analyze, build, test, and present their solution to a real-world engineering problem. The mentors guide the students through the design and prototyping process and coach students on professional skills like technical communication, presentations and teamwork. The program also includes career counseling and college planning sessions with Cooper Union staff members.

Partnering Schools

Partnering Schools

Cooper Union undergraduates teach these modules under the guidance of faculty members and technical staff. The STEM Day experience is designed in collaboration with the partnering school such that it aligns with what is being taught in their science and math classes. In the past, some schools have opted for a tour of the laboratories and a lecture – both representing their students’ first introduction to the STEM fields outside the classroom. Other schools have had their students perform experiments in an engineering laboratory. For some schools, visiting Cooper Union is not feasible due to scheduling, busing, and funding. In these cases, Cooper Union students have visited the partnering schools with design and experimentation kits – we call this trip STEM-To-Go. Currently, Cooper is partnered with LaSalle Academy and Grace Church Highschool.

STEM Days

STEM Days

During a STEM Day program, fifth through twelfth graders are immersed in one-day, intensive experiences that expose them to the diversity and impact of the STEM fields on modern society. These field trips to Cooper Union start with a tour of the New Academic Building at 41 Cooper Square, where the synthesis of art, architecture, and engineering are discussed in terms of the design trade-offs that are struck as practitioners collaborate to build multidisciplinary projects of this scale.

- Makerspace Guidelines -

  • If you would like to 3D print something, please fill out the form, which can be found below.
  • When your part is printing/finished, we will contact you with pick up instructions
  • If the print is too complicated and takes too long to print, we will contact you.
  • Please allow at least 7 days before a print can be complete.
  • Max size allowed to print is 210mm x 160mm x 210mm
  • Please try to make it no taller than 100 mm and less than 80% of the maximum print volume.
  • We do not take color requests
  • If the object looks 2D, consider laser cutting instead
  • If you would like to lasercut, please contact Lima Lab.

Some tips for 3D printing:

  • Very tall objects will take more time and have a greater chance of failing.
  • Very thin parts are unable to print.
  • The format of the file sent should be STL saved in mm.
  • Anything that has an angle 45 degrees or larger, in reference to the x-axis, will not need support when being printed.
  • When creating holes, the tolerance should be 0.8 mm greater for the inner diameter.
